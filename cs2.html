calendar

task

Reconceptualize a familiar object: the calendar, demonstrating an ability to provide a fresh take on a traditional design. Integrate digital and analog processes to create a clean product.

idea

Beyond simply conceptualizing the monthly calendar in an alternative way, I settled on a theme with relevance to my daily routine—one that would allow me to convey my love for personal expression. Then, I set out to create a rotating eyeshadow palette representing February 2020.

approach

first

I spent a good amount of time looking to existing eyeshadow palettes for inspiration.

A series of choices had to be made with regard to the material, scale and color schemes—I decided to undertake laser cutting the palette out of acrylic, rendering it in a high-contrast red-white color harmony.

second

As a level of functionality was necessary for the project to be successful, I had to be thoughtful when engineering the lazy Susan form.

After researching acrylic's limitations and nature, I developed a design with an acrylic dowel and acrylic washers to support rotation.

third

I wanted my project to not only be functional, but to deliver aesthetically as well—I iterated through several graphic designs for the top layer of the palette, eventually rendering my final design digitally.

Once I took my design into the digital workspace, I spent a good amount of time finalizing it before actually laser cutting. I only had one shot at it, and I wanted it to be good.

fourth

Finally, it was simply a matter of assembly and fine-tuning. I spray painted the etched lines to polish off the graphic design, and used adhesive putty to attach the eyeshadow singles to the lower layer. There it was—February 2020 represented in eyeshadow.
